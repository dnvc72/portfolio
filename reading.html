Classic Literature

Classic Literature is something I've always grown up with, and as an Omega student, I continue to use lessons and writing styles from it in my work.
I grew up reading works by the likes of Shakespeare, Oscar Wilde, and Charles Dickens, and more recently watching their screen adaptations. Something to appreciate about classic literature is the extensive vocabulary, elaborate metaphors and symbolism, and moral lessons. Reading classic literature is like trying to solve a puzzle, and something you can read again and again, to new details and meanings in.

Works of the Brontë Sisters

My Faves
  1. Wuthering Heights (by Emily)
  2. Jane Eyre (by Charlotte)
  3. Agnes Grey (by Anne)
Unlike many other authors of the time, the Brontë family of writers was unafraid to write dramatic, unsettling, gothic-style novels, allowing their works to stand the test of time and continue to pique readers' interest. Because female authors were not taken seriously in the 19th century, each sister had pseudonyms matching their initials.


Works of Jane Austen

My Faves
  1. Emma
  2. Pride & Prejudice
  3. Sense & Sensibility
Why does Jane Austen's work remain relevant today? Could it be its themes of sacrifice, female autonomy, and sisterhood? The Austen movie fever in the 90s gave us adaptations like Sense and Sensibility with Kate Winslet, Pride and Prejudice with Colin Firth, and the modern retelling of Emma: Clueless, starring Alicia Silverstone. This era of film showcases the enduring appeal of her stories, due to her ability to capture the complexities of relationships and human nature.


Works of Shakespeare

My Faves
  1. Much Ado About Nothing
  2. A Midsummer's Night Dream
  3. Taming of the Shrew
Many remember Shakespeare as something required to read for school, but I remember it as nonstop riddles and wordplay, whimsical movies, and stories that truly make you question the fragility of human nature. Another similarly witty and critiquing playright is Oscar Wilde, and his play, The Importance of Being Earnest is amongst my favorites to read.


Works of Charles Dickens

My Faves
  1. Nicholas Nickleby.
  2. David Copperfield
  3. Great Expectations
Charles Dickens' works expose the harsh realities of Victorian society, or any society organized by class and status. His stories highlight the universal themes of resilience, redemption, and the pursuit of justice. They're more bittersweet reads, focusing on issues of poverty, class struggle, and social injustice that is sometimes never paid back. E.M. Forster's A Room With a View is from a similar era of writing, and my favorite book of all time. More recent, similar works focused on moral lessons, such as Atonement by Ian McEwan, are also on top.
